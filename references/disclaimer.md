# Disclaimer reference

The single most important thing this skill produces is the right disclaimer language in the right places. Regulators, courts, users, and lawyers will all check this before anything else.

## Three layers - each must be present

### Layer 1 - skill-level warning (shown to the USER at the start of every run)

Surface this message to the user BEFORE proceeding with Phase 0 intake:

```
WARNING - NOT LEGAL ADVICE

This skill drafts privacy policies, terms of service, and related
compliance documents based on your codebase + research + peer-SaaS
benchmarks. It is NOT a substitute for a licensed attorney in your
jurisdiction.

You MUST have a qualified lawyer review the output before relying on
it in production, especially if any of these apply:

- You charge users (paid product, subscriptions, one-time purchase)
- You have EU, UK, or California users at scale
- You process health, financial, biometric, or children's data
- You sign enterprise B2B contracts with a DPA clause
- You are responding to a regulator, a subpoena, or a complaint

The primary-source citations the skill provides help your attorney's
review; they are not a substitute for it.

Proceed? (yes / no / explain)
```

Do not proceed until the user acknowledges. If they skip acknowledgement or push back, remind them gently and ask again.

### Layer 2 - document-level disclaimer (shipped AT THE TOP of every generated policy)

Include this as a short block near the start of /privacy and /terms:

```
About this policy

This policy is drafted by the operator and (optionally) reviewed by
counsel. It is designed to describe what we actually do, in plain
English, and to satisfy the transparency requirements of the EU GDPR,
UK GDPR, California CCPA/CPRA, US COPPA, and the Israel Privacy
Protection Law (PPL). If anything below is unclear or feels wrong,
email {legal_email}.

Last Updated: {DATE}.
```

If professional counsel HAS reviewed the output, the operator may additionally state "Reviewed by counsel on {date}." Do not add this line unless the user confirmed in Phase 0 that a lawyer actually reviewed it.

### Layer 3 - README-level disclaimer (visible on GitHub, in the install docs, in the skill card)

```
Not legal advice.

This skill is an AI-powered document-drafting assistant. The policies,
agreements, and assessments it generates are produced in a fully
automated manner from your inputs and should be understood as
first-draft working documents, not as legal advice. They do not
substitute for review by an attorney licensed in your jurisdiction(s).
You remain solely responsible for the accuracy, completeness, and
legal suitability of any document you publish. Before relying on
output from this skill, have it reviewed by qualified counsel. No
attorney-client relationship is formed by your use of this skill.
```

Adapted from industry-standard language used by Iubenda, Termly, and similar privacy-policy tooling. It is deliberately emphatic because the alternative - a user pasting AI-drafted legal text into production without review - is how regulator fines and class actions happen.

## When to refuse

The skill must REFUSE to produce output and redirect the user to counsel, in these situations:

- The user explicitly asks the skill to replace their lawyer.
- The user is facing an active regulatory inquiry, DSAR deadline, or litigation. The skill is not adversarial-process-ready.
- The user works in a heavily regulated domain (banking, healthcare-PHI, insurance, minors-first product) where domain-specific rules (HIPAA, GLBA, SOX, COPPA-plus) require specialist review.
- The user operates in a jurisdiction this skill does not cover (e.g., China PIPL, India DPDP, Brazil LGPD as primary). Refuse politely and suggest a firm that specializes.

Refusal template:

```
This request is outside the safe scope of this skill. Specifically,
{reason}. I can help you prepare a brief for your attorney - a summary
of your data flows, sub-processors, and the questions counsel needs to
answer - but I will not draft the policy itself for this case.

Want me to prepare the attorney-brief instead?
```

## Recency marker

Every generated policy file should contain a machine-readable recency marker:

```html
<!--
  Generated by compliance-auditor on {DATE}.
  Regulatory guidance changes. Re-run this skill:
  - At minimum, every 6 months
  - When you add or remove a sub-processor
  - When you change jurisdictions (open EU marketing, expand to UK, etc.)
  - When a significant regulator action hits one of your sub-processors
-->
```

This is a hard signal to future maintainers that the file is not static and needs periodic re-review.
